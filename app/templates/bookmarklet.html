JD Capture bookmarklet

A one-click bookmark that grabs the visible JD text from whatever job page you're looking at — including LinkedIn, Workday, Ashby, Indeed, and any internal career site — and POSTs it to this local app for screening. Because it runs inside your own browser, you're already logged in to LinkedIn etc., so auth is never a problem.

1. Install

Make sure your browser's bookmarks bar is visible (⌘⇧B in Chrome). Drag the button below to the bar:

📋 Capture JD → Job Search Kit

2. Use it

  1. Open any job posting in your browser (LinkedIn, Workday, company site, anything).
  2. Click the Capture JD bookmark.
  3. A new tab opens with the New Application form pre-filled with the JD text. Review, edit the company / role if needed, then click Screen this JD.

Troubleshooting

  • Nothing happens after click: the bookmarklet's target is {{ base_url }}. If your app runs on a different port, regenerate the bookmarklet from this page after restarting on the new port.
  • "Blocked by CSP": some sites (notably some Workday and single-tenant ATS deployments) have strict Content-Security-Policy headers that prevent the bookmarklet from injecting a form. Fall back to selecting the JD text manually, copy, and paste into the New Application textarea.
  • Captured too much / too little text: the bookmarklet tries common JD container selectors first and falls back to the whole body. You can always trim or replace the textarea content before screening.

What it captures

Page URL, document.title, and the inner text of the first matching JD container (LinkedIn's .jobs-description__content, Workday's [data-automation-id=jobPostingDescription], etc.) — falling back to the full body. Everything stays on your machine: it POSTs only to {{ base_url }}/import.
